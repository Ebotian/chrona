Loving v. Virginia affected other civil rights movements by providing a legal precedent and a moral boost for challenging racial discrimination and segregation in various areas of society. The decision also inspired other groups, such as LGBTQ+ activists, to fight for their own rights to marry and love whom they choose¹. Some of the specific impacts of Loving v. Virginia on other civil rights movements are:

- It helped invalidate the remaining anti-miscegenation laws in 16 states, mostly in the South².
- It influenced the passage of the Immigration and Nationality Act of 1965, which abolished the national origins quota system that restricted immigration based on race and ethnicity³.
- It supported the claims of Native Americans who sought federal recognition of their tribal marriages and sovereignty rights⁴.
- It paved the way for the legalization of same-sex marriage in Obergefell v. Hodges (2015), which cited Loving v. Virginia as a key precedent¹.
